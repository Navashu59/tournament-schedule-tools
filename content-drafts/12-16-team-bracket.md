# 16 Team Bracket

## Tool Intro
Set up a 16 team bracket in seconds. No signup needed. Enter your teams, seed them, and get a printable single-elimination schedule with rounds, times, and venues.

## How To Use It
1. Enter 16 team names (or fewer — the tool adds byes automatically).
2. Assign seeds if you want fair matchups (1 vs 16, 2 vs 15, etc.).
3. Add optional time slots, courts, or venues per match.
4. Click generate. Your bracket appears instantly.
5. Print, copy the schedule, or export as CSV.

## Example
You run a weekend basketball tournament with 12 teams. Enter the 12 names. The tool adds 4 byes (teams that skip Round 1). Seed your top 4 teams to face the byes. The bracket shows all rounds, winners advancing, and a final champion.

## What The Results Mean
- **Round 1**: 8 matches (or fewer with byes). Winners move on.
- **Quarterfinals**: 4 matches. Winners go to semis.
- **Semifinals**: 2 matches. Winners play the final.
- **Final**: 1 match. Winner is champion.
- **Total games**: 15 (for 16 teams) or fewer if you have byes.

## Common Mistakes
- **Forgetting byes**: If you have fewer than 16 teams, byes are required. The tool adds them, but you must place seeded teams against byes to keep fairness.
- **Wrong seed order**: Seed 1 plays the lowest seed (or a bye). Seed 2 plays the second lowest. Mixing this up creates unfair matchups early.
- **No time buffer**: Games run long. Add 15–20 minutes between time slots.

## Common Questions

**How do you make a tournament bracket?**
Enter your teams, choose single elimination, set seeds if needed, and generate. The tool handles rounds and matchups.

**How do you seed a bracket?**
Rank teams 1 (best) to 16 (worst). The tool pairs 1 vs 16, 2 vs 15, etc. This rewards top teams with easier first-round games.

**What is a bye?**
A free pass to the next round. Used when you have fewer than 16 teams. Byes go to the highest seeds so they don't play Round 1.

**How many games are in a single elimination bracket?**
Always (teams – 1). For 16 teams: 15 games. For 12 teams: 11 games (plus byes).

**What is the difference between single and double elimination?**
Single elimination: lose once, you're out. Double elimination: lose once, you drop to a losers bracket; lose twice, you're out. Double elimination runs more games.
